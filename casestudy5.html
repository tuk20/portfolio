Case Study: NATURIZE

Preparing for the U.S. Citizenship Test

Project Overview

This project was part of the Google UX Design Professional Certificate program on Coursera. Inspired by my lifelong learner mom and the challenges faced by immigrants and refugees in my community, I designed NATURIZE, a mobile app to help users prepare for the U.S. citizenship test. The app aims to provide a comprehensive, engaging, and personalized learning experience to improve users' chances of passing the naturalization test.

Project Duration: 2 weeks (detail breakdown provided in the process section).

The Problem

The Immigration and Nationality Act (INA) mandates that naturalization applicants pass a test with English and civics components. However, only 88% of applicants pass on their first attempt, and many immigrants and refugees struggle to pass even on subsequent attempts. Common challenges include:

  • Lack of access to structured and engaging study materials.
  • Difficulty managing time and anxiety during preparation.
  • Limited personalized feedback to address individual learning gaps.

This problem inspired me to create a solution that empowers immigrants and refugees to confidently prepare for and pass the citizenship test.

The Goal

The goal of this project is to design a mobile app that:

  • Provides comprehensive and engaging learning materials for the U.S. citizenship test.
  • Offers personalized feedback to address individual learning needs.
  • Includes social support features to foster a sense of community.
  • Tracks user progress and readiness for the test.

My Responsibilities

  • Conducting user research (interviews, surveys, and competitive analysis).
  • Defining the problem and project goals.
  • Creating user personas, empathy maps, and user journey maps.
  • Designing user flows, wireframes (lo-fi and hi-fi), and prototypes.
  • Conducting usability testing and gathering insights.
  • Iterating on designs based on feedback.

Research

User Research

I conducted user interviews with 2 immigrants and refugees who had taken or were preparing for the citizenship test. Key insights included:

  • Users felt overwhelmed by the volume of material to study.
  • Many struggled with anxiety and time management.
  • Users wanted interactive and engaging ways to learn, such as games and flashcards.
  • Personalized feedback and progress tracking were highly desired.

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed existing citizenship test preparation apps and identified gaps:

  • Most apps lacked engaging and interactive features.
  • Few offered personalized learning paths or social support.
  • Many had outdated designs and poor usability.

Personas

Based on the research, I created two personas to represent the target users:

  • Maria, 35, Immigrant from Mexico: Struggles with English proficiency, feels anxious about the test, and has limited time to study due to work and family responsibilities.
  • Ahmed, 28, Refugee from Egypt: Finds the civics material overwhelming and lacks access to structured study resources.

Design Process

Empathy Maps

I created empathy maps to better understand users' thoughts, feelings, and pain points. This helped me prioritize features that address their emotional and practical needs.

Says

  • "I feel overwhelmed by the amount of material I need to study."
  • "I wish there were more interactive ways to learn."
  • "I need help managing my time effectively."

Thinks

  • "What if I fail the test again?"
  • "I hope this app can help me stay organized."
  • "I wonder if there are others like me struggling with this."

Feels

  • Anxious about the test format.
  • Frustrated with traditional study methods.
  • Hopeful that this app will make a difference.

Does

  • Spends hours reading textbooks but retains little.
  • Seeks out online resources but finds them disorganized.
  • Joins study groups but struggles to keep up.

User Journey Map

I mapped out the user journey to identify pain points and opportunities for improvement. Key stages included:

  • Discovering the app.
  • Setting up a study plan.
  • Engaging with learning materials.
  • Tracking progress and receiving feedback.

1. Discovery

  • Learns about the app from a friend.
  • Visits the app store to download NATURIZE.
  • Reads reviews and feels hopeful.

2. Onboarding

  • Completes the sign-up process.
  • Sets up a profile with personal details.
  • Takes a placement test to assess their level.

3. Learning

  • Engages with flashcards and quizzes.
  • Watches educational videos and listens to audio clips.
  • Participates in interactive games to reinforce learning.

4. Progress Tracking

  • Views progress reports and test readiness scores.
  • Receives personalized feedback and study tips.
  • Adjusts study plan based on recommendations.

5. Community Support

  • Joins study groups and forums.
  • Shares experiences and tips with other users.
  • Feels motivated by the sense of community.

6. Test Preparation

  • Completes mock tests to simulate the exam.
  • Reviews weak areas identified by the app.
  • Feels confident and ready for the citizenship test.

Information Architecture

I designed the app's information architecture to ensure intuitive navigation and easy access to key features, such as study materials, progress tracking, and community support.

Wireframes

Paper Wireframes

I started with paper wireframes to quickly iterate on ideas for the app's homepage and main features. The goal was to create a captivating hero image and ensure the layout was user-friendly.

Digital Wireframes

Based on the paper wireframes, I created a digital wireframe for the home page, incorporating all the features I find appealing in the paper wireframes, focusing on usability and visual hierarchy.

User Flow

I designed the main user flow to ensure a seamless experience, from onboarding to completing a study session.

The Solution: NATURIZE

NATURIZE is a mobile app that prepares applicants for the U.S. citizenship test through personalized and engaging learning. Key features include:

  • Adaptive Learning: The app adapts to the userâ€™s level and pace.
  • Diverse Learning Tools: Questions, flashcards, games, videos, and audio clips cater to different learning styles.
  • Personalized Feedback: Explanations, tips, and tricks help users improve.
  • Progress Tracking: Users can monitor their readiness for the test.
  • Social Support: A community feature allows users to connect and share experiences.

Usability Testing

I conducted usability testing with 5 participants to gather feedback on the app's design and functionality. Key findings included:

  • Users appreciated the interactive features but wanted more variety in games.
  • Some found the progress tracking feature confusing.
  • The onboarding process needed to be more intuitive.

Based on this feedback, I iterated on the design to improve usability and address user concerns.

Takeaways

Designing and prototyping NATURIZE in two weeks was a challenging but rewarding experience. Key lessons included:

  • The importance of setting clear goals and deadlines for rapid design projects.
  • The value of user feedback in refining designs and improving usability.
  • Areas for improvement: Seeking more feedback from experienced UX designers and exploring additional features like AI-driven personalized learning.

Future Improvements

  • Add more interactive features, such as quizzes and leaderboards, to increase engagement.
  • Improve the onboarding process to make it more user-friendly.
  • Conduct additional usability testing with a larger and more diverse group of users.
  • Explore partnerships with organizations that support immigrants and refugees to increase the app's reach.
