The Problem & Constraints

  • Client was not tech-savvy and needed something that "just worked" without future maintenance.
  • Must present all essential campaign content (bio, platform, donation, volunteer, contact) in a single-scroll format.
  • Must work flawlessly on mobile as the target audience includes older voters using smartphones.

Design & Development Process

Discovery & Research

  • Conducted client interviews to define primary goals: voter trust, visibility, and ease of access
  • Researched successful political campaign websites for layout inspiration and keyword targets

Wireframing & Prototyping

  • Created low and high-fidelity wireframes in Figma
  • Prioritized a top-down hierarchy: Hero section with CTA, platform, biography, volunteer, donate, contact

Development & SEO

  • Built responsive site using GoDaddy CMS drag-and-drop editor with custom HTML/CSS overrides
  • Submitted sitemap to Google Search Console for indexing
  • Integrated political SEO keywords (e.g., "[District] 2025 election," "vote for [Candidate Name]")

Accessibility & Performance

  • Tested using WAVE and Lighthouse tools
  • Achieved 100% accessibility compliance and high performance scores on mobile

Results & Impact

  • Website indexed on Google within 48 hours of launch
  • Client reported positive voter feedback about ease of use
  • Mobile visits accounted for 70% of total traffic within the first two weeks
  • No technical support needed post-launch

Key Takeaways

  • Designing for non-technical clients means optimizing both UX and backend simplicity
  • Clear visual hierarchy and single-page layouts are powerful when space is limited
  • Pre-launch SEO and mobile-first design lead to immediate engagement gains
